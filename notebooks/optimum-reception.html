Optimum Reception

June 05, 2017

When we transmit a signal it gets distorted due to different reasons. So there should be a method to identify the transmitted signal at the reciever end. In this article we simulate identifying a randomly generated binary signal sequence which is corrupted by Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) using a simple decision rule and demonstrate how Bit Error Rate (BER) and $ Q\left(\sqrt{\frac{E_s}{N_0}}\right)$ varies with $ \frac{E_s}{N_0}$ value. Furthermore we investigate the relationship between BER and $ Q\left(\sqrt{\frac{E_s}{N_0}}\right)$.

This result is no wonder but a mere application of probability theory. We can show it as follows:

As the name suggests bit error rate (BER) is the relative frequency of occurring an error. (See the Appendix for more details.) It is a fundamental fact in probability theory that as the number of trials tends to infinity, the relative frequency of an event reaches the probability of occurring that event. Since the number of bits in our bit sequence is large enough ($10^5$), we can safely say that BER is nothing but the probability of occurring an error at the receiver, $P(E)$. We now show that $P(E)=Q\left(\sqrt{\frac{E_s}{N_0}}\right)$.

$$ \begin{align*} P(E)&=P(E\cap\boldsymbol {s_1})+P(E\cap\boldsymbol {s_2})\\ &=P(E|\boldsymbol {s_1})P(\boldsymbol{s_1})+P(E|\boldsymbol {s_2})P(\boldsymbol{s_2}) \end{align*}$$

As $P(\boldsymbol{s_1})=P(\boldsymbol{s_2})=\frac{1}{2}$ and $P(E|\boldsymbol {s_1})=P(E|\boldsymbol {s_2})$

$$P(E)=P(E|\boldsymbol{s_1})$$

By looking at Figure, we can show that,

$$P(E|\boldsymbol{s_1})=P\left(\Im(\boldsymbol {s_1}+\boldsymbol n)>\Re(\boldsymbol {s_1}+\boldsymbol n)\right)$$

Which yields,

$$P(E)=P\left(\boldsymbol {n_C}-\boldsymbol{n_R}>\sqrt{E_s}\right)$$

Since,

$$\boldsymbol{n_c}-\boldsymbol{n_R}\sim N(0,N_0)$$

the probability of error becomes,

$$\begin{align*} P(E)&=P\left(\frac{\overline{\boldsymbol{n_C}-\boldsymbol{n_R}}-0}{\sqrt{N_0}}>\sqrt{\frac{E_s}{N_0}}\right)\\ &=Q\left(\sqrt{\frac{E_s}{N_0}}\right) \end{align*}$$
